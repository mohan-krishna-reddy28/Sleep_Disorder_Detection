About NeuroSleep System

Project Overview

NeuroSleep is an AI-powered sleep disorder detection system capable of identifying three conditions: Insomnia, Sleep Apnea, and No Disorder. It uses machine learning and deep learning models trained on health and lifestyle data.

How NeuroSleep Works

1. Enter Your Health & Lifestyle Details

Provide basic information about your sleep duration, stress level, physical activity, heart rate, and other daily habits.

2. AI Models Analyze Your Data

Our Machine Learning & Deep Learning models (RF, CNN, LSTM) evaluate your inputs to identify hidden patterns related to Insomnia & Sleep Apnea.

3. Get Instant Sleep Disorder Prediction

The system instantly generates your predicted sleep condition along with lifestyle recommendations to improve your sleep health.

How the Models Work

Random Forest

Random Forest is an ensemble learning model that builds multiple decision trees and combines their outputs. It performs extremely well for tabular data and is the most accurate model for this dataset (96% accuracy).

CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)

CNNs extract patterns using convolutional filters and are powerful for feature learning. In NeuroSleep, CNN learns relationships between multiple health indicators to classify sleep disorders.

LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory)

LSTM is designed for sequential data and time dependencies. In NeuroSleep, it captures patterns in sequential-like health metrics to detect disorders.

Form Input Requirements

  • Age (Number) – Expected as a numeric value (e.g., 18–80). Sleep patterns strongly vary with age.
  • Gender (Male/Female) – Encoded internally as numeric labels. Required because sleep disorders affect genders differently.
  • Occupation (Category) – Converted to encoded numeric form. Helps the model understand lifestyle-based stress.
  • Sleep Duration (Hours, Number) – Model expects values like 4.0–10.0 hours.
  • Quality of Sleep (1–10) – User rating; higher values mean better sleep.
  • Physical Activity Level (1–100) – Higher activity levels generally improve sleep.
  • Stress Level (1–10) – Higher values indicate more stress, affecting sleep quality.
  • BMI Category (Underweight / Normal / Overweight / Obese) – Internally converted to numeric labels. Obesity strongly correlates with Sleep Apnea.
  • Systolic Blood Pressure (Number) – Model expects the upper BP value (e.g., 110–160).
  • Diastolic Blood Pressure (Number) – Model expects the lower BP value (e.g., 70–100).
  • Heart Rate (BPM, Number) – Typically ranges from 60–110 during rest. Higher rates may indicate stress or poor sleep.
  • Daily Steps (Number) – Indicates physical movement; affects sleep patterns.
  • Model Type (RF / CNN / LSTM) – Lets user choose prediction model. RF = Best accuracy, CNN = Deep learning patterns, LSTM = Sequential analysis.
